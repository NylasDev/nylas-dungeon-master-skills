# Example: solo campaign start

This example uses original setting material. It demonstrates how `agent-dungeon-master` starts play without copying a rulebook, adventure, stat block, or protected setting.

## OOC assumptions

- System: D&D-style fantasy, rules-light until mechanics matter.
- Player count: one hero.
- Tone: heroic danger with strange coastal mystery.
- Difficulty: heroic mode. Failure has teeth, but the first session will not end the campaign on one bad roll.
- Safety: no graphic sexual content, no sexual violence, no real-world hate as fantasy seasoning.

## Three campaign premises

1. **The bell under the tide**
   A drowned temple bell rings once every new moon. Each time it rings, someone in the fishing town of Halewick forgets a name they loved.

2. **The prince of rusted antlers**
   Iron antlers grow from trees across the borderlands. Hunters who cut them down hear a voice asking for its crown back.

3. **The lighthouse that eats ships**
   A dead lighthouse shines with black flame during storms. Ships do not crash there. They vanish, then return years later with strangers wearing the crews' faces.

The player chooses premise 1.

## Opening scene

**Scene:** Halewick's salt pier groans under your boots. The tide is out farther than anyone remembers, exposing black stone steps that should be deep beneath the bay. At the bottom, something bronze and enormous glints in the moonlight.

**What you notice:**

- Every dog in town is silent.
- Old Mara Venn, the harbor keeper, is crying because she cannot remember her son's name.
- A wet rope lies across the steps. It is moving against the wind.

**Pressure:** The new moon is nearly overhead. If the drowned bell rings again, someone else will lose a memory.

**Obvious options:**

- Go down the exposed steps before the tide turns.
- Question Mara Venn about the last bell ring.
- Inspect the moving rope from a safer distance.

What do you do?

## Sample player action

> I go down the steps, but I tie myself to a pier post first. I want a way back if the tide snaps in.

## Sample ruling

OOC ruling: Good precaution. No roll needed to tie the rope if you take the time. The risky part is descending the slick steps before the tide shifts.

Stakes: Make an agility-style check. On success, you reach the bronze shape cleanly. On a partial result, you reach it but something below notices the rope. On failure, you slip and lose time as the tide begins to move.

## Sample consequence

You descend one careful step at a time. Salt crystals crunch under your boots. Halfway down, the moving rope near the bottom rises like a snake.

It is not rope. It is kelp braided around a human wristbone.

Your own safety line goes taut behind you. Something under the pier has found it.

What do you do?

## Private DM state - do not reveal during play

- Hidden clock: Bell rings at 6/6. Starts at 3/6 because the moon is already high.
- NPC secret: Mara traded her son's name to save him from drowning years ago.
- Unrevealed clue: The bell steals names to keep a sealed thing asleep.
- Faction move: The tide-priests under the bay want the hero to ring the bell willingly.
- Spotlight target: Reward caution and curiosity. The safety rope should matter without solving the danger.
